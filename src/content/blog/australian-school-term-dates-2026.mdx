School terms are the backbone of most Australian parenting arrangements. Whether your parenting order says "alternate weekends during term time" or "the first half of each school holiday period with Parent A," knowing exactly when terms start and end is essential.

The challenge is that every state and territory sets its own school calendar. Term 1 might start on 27 January in Victoria but 5 February in Tasmania. A two-week difference might not sound like much, but when you are coordinating handovers, booking flights, or arranging care during school holidays, those dates matter.

Here are the confirmed 2026 school term dates for every Australian state and territory, along with key state-specific holidays that affect the school calendar.

## New South Wales

| Term | Start | End |
|------|-------|-----|
| Term 1 | Monday 2 February | Thursday 2 April |
| Term 2 | Wednesday 22 April | Friday 3 July |
| Term 3 | Tuesday 21 July | Friday 25 September |
| Term 4 | Tuesday 13 October | Thursday 17 December |

**Notes**: NSW has separate dates for Western Division schools (remote western NSW), which start Term 1 one week later on Monday 9 February. If you are co-parenting across the Eastern and Western divisions, check which calendar your child's school follows. Staff development days (also called pupil-free days) are set by individual schools and fall at the start or end of each term.

**Key state holiday**: Bank Holiday (first Monday in August) applies to some financial sector workers but is not a general public holiday.

## How school terms affect parenting arrangements

If you have a parenting order or parenting plan, it almost certainly references school terms. Here are the most common patterns and how term dates affect them:

### Term-time arrangements

Many orders specify a regular schedule during school terms (for example, "every second weekend from Friday after school to Monday morning") and a different schedule during school holidays. Knowing exactly when terms start and end tells you when to switch between schedules.

### School holiday splits

Orders commonly divide school holidays between parents - either by alternating each holiday period or splitting each holiday in half. The exact start and end dates of each holiday determine the handover points.

A common arrangement is: "The children spend the first half of each school holiday period with Parent A and the second half with Parent B, alternating each year." To calculate the midpoint, you need the exact last day of term and the exact first day of the next term.

### Long weekends and public holidays

State-specific public holidays that fall during term time create long weekends. These may be specifically addressed in your parenting order, or they may follow your regular term-time schedule. Either way, you need to know when they fall.

### Interstate co-parenting

If you and your co-parent live in different states, your children's school calendar follows the state where they attend school - not the state where the other parent lives. This can create complications when the non-school parent's state has different holiday dates.

## Tips for managing school holiday handovers

### 1. Confirm dates with your child's school

The dates in this guide are for government schools. Independent and Catholic schools often set slightly different term dates. Your child's school will publish its specific calendar - use those dates, not generic state dates, for your handover planning.

### 2. Agree on handover logistics early

Do not wait until the last day of term to sort out pickup arrangements. Ideally, confirm handover details at least two weeks before each school holiday period begins.

### 3. Account for pupil-free days

Many schools have staff development or pupil-free days at the start or end of each term. Your child might finish school a day earlier or start a day later than the official term dates suggest. Check your school's specific calendar.

### 4. Plan for travel during holidays

If either parent plans to travel with the children during school holidays, most parenting orders require advance notice (commonly 14 to 28 days). Start those conversations early.

### 5. Use a shared digital calendar

A shared calendar that both parents can see avoids miscommunication about dates. When both parents are looking at the same calendar with the same school terms loaded, there are fewer opportunities for confusion.

## A note about accuracy

The dates in this guide are based on information published by state and territory education departments as of April 2026. However:

- **Independent and Catholic schools** may have different term dates. Always check with your child's specific school.
- **Dates can change**. State governments occasionally adjust school calendars. Check your state education department's website for the most up-to-date information.
- **Pupil-free days** vary by school and are not included in these tables.
